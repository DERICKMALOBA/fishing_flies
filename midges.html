About Midge Flies

Midges are among the smallest insects that trout feed upon, yet they represent one of the most important food sources year-round, especially during winter months when larger insects are absent. These tiny Chironomid flies are found in virtually every body of water that holds trout, and fish often feed on them with maddening selectivity. Having properly tied midge patterns in sizes 18-26 is essential for any serious trout angler.

Our midge collection features patterns that have proven themselves on the most demanding waters. The Zebra Midge is a simple yet devastatingly effective pattern that imitates midge larvae and pupae with its segmented body and subtle flash from the silver wire rib. The Brassie uses copper wire to create weight and segmentation, perfect for fishing deep in the water column. The WD-40 is a versatile midge emerger that works in the surface film when fish are sipping tiny adults. Each midge pattern is tied with precision on quality small-wire hooks, featuring proportionally correct materials that present a realistic profile to discerning trout.
