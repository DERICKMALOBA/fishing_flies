Nymph Flies

About Nymph Flies

Nymph flies imitate aquatic insects in their underwater larval and pupal stages, representing the food source that trout feed on approximately 80-90% of the time. Unlike dry flies that float on the surface, nymphs are designed to sink and drift naturally along the stream bottom where fish actively hunt for food. Mastering nymph fishing is essential for any serious fly angler looking to catch fish consistently throughout the year.

Our nymph collection features legendary patterns that have proven themselves on waters worldwide. The Pheasant Tail Nymph, originally tied by Frank Sawyer, imitates a wide range of mayfly nymphs with its slender profile and natural pheasant tail fibers. The Gold-Ribbed Hare's Ear creates an irresistible buggy appearance with its fuzzy dubbing that pulses with life in the current. The Prince Nymph, with its distinctive white biot wings, serves as an excellent attractor pattern when fish aren't keyed in on specific insects. Each nymph is tied on quality hooks with properly weighted bodies to ensure they reach the strike zone quickly and drift naturally through the water column.
