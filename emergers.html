Emerger Flies

About Emerger Flies

Emerger flies represent one of the most critical stages in an aquatic insect's life cycle - the vulnerable moment when nymphs or pupae ascend from the stream bottom to transform into winged adults at the surface. During emergence, insects become trapped in the surface film, struggling to break free of their shucks, making them easy targets for feeding trout. Understanding and imitating this transitional stage is crucial for fooling selective fish.

Our emerger collection includes patterns designed to fish in the surface film where the magic happens. The RS2 is a minimalist emerger that has proven deadly on tailwaters and spring creeks across the world. CDC Emergers utilize the natural water-repellent properties of CDC feathers to float perfectly in the film while presenting a realistic silhouette. The Klinkhammer, designed by Hans van Klinken, features a curved body that hangs below the surface while the parachute hackle keeps it visible and afloat. These patterns are essential during any mayfly, caddis, or midge emergence when trout are sipping seemingly invisible insects from the surface.
