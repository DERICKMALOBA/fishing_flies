About Streamer Flies

Streamer flies imitate baitfish, leeches, crayfish, and other larger prey that big trout actively hunt. Unlike nymphs and dry flies that rely on dead-drift presentations, streamers are actively stripped through the water to trigger aggressive predatory strikes. When you want to target the largest fish in a river or lake, streamers are your go-to patterns. They also excel during high water, low light conditions, and whenever fish are in an aggressive feeding mood.

Our streamer collection includes time-tested patterns that have fooled countless trophy fish. The Woolly Bugger may be the most versatile fly ever created, imitating everything from leeches to sculpins depending on how it's fished. The Muddler Minnow features a spun deer hair head that pushes water and creates an irresistible disturbance. The Clouser Minnow's weighted eyes give it a jigging action that drives predatory fish wild. Each streamer is tied on strong hooks with durable materials designed to withstand the violent strikes and sharp teeth of aggressive fish.
