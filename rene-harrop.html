About Rene Harrop Patterns

Rene Harrop is a world-renowned fly tier and fly fishing legend, best known for his work on Idaho's Henry's Fork of the Snake River. His patterns are celebrated for their elegant simplicity and incredible effectiveness on technical waters where trout are notoriously selective. Rene pioneered the use of CDC (Cul de Canard) feathers in American fly tying and developed patterns that have become standards in technical dry fly fishing.

Our Rene Harrop collection features his most influential patterns, including his legendary CDC-based designs that float perfectly in the surface film with a natural, lifelike appearance. From his innovative emergers to his precise mayfly imitations, these patterns represent the pinnacle of technical fly design. Each fly is tied with careful attention to the proportions and materials that make Harrop's patterns so effective on demanding spring creeks and tailwaters worldwide.
